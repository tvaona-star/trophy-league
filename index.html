World Cup 2026 — Tournament Format

48 teams compete across the United States, Canada, and Mexico from June 11 to July 19, 2026 — the biggest World Cup ever.

Group stage (June 11–27): 12 groups (A–L) of 4 teams. Each team plays 3 group matches. The top 2 in each group plus the 8 best third-place teams advance — 32 teams move on.

Knockout rounds: Round of 32 → Round of 16 → Quarterfinals → Semifinals. Single elimination, extra time and penalties if needed.

Third-place match (July 18): The two semifinal losers play one final match to decide 3rd and 4th place.

Final (July 19): MetLife Stadium, New Jersey. Winner lifts the trophy.

Trophy League — How the Draft Game Works

Step 1 — The Spin (before June 11): The wheel decides World Cup draft order. The first name drawn gets WC Pick #10 (last to choose), counting down — the final name left gets Pick #1. Don't blame the wheel, blame your luck.

Step 2 — The Nation Draft: 3-round snake draft. Round 1 goes picks 1→10, Round 2 reverses 10→1, Round 3 goes 1→10. Every manager ends up with 3 of the 48 nations. Once a nation is taken, it's gone.

Step 3 — The Tournament: Your 3 nations earn a placement score based on how far they go. Lower is better:

Final score: the average of your 3 teams' placement scores. Lowest average earns fantasy Pick #1; highest average gets stuck with Pick #10.

Tiebreakers, in order: ① best single team finish among your 3 ② most combined goals scored by your 3 teams across the whole tournament ③ whoever picked later in the World Cup snake draft.

Note: there is no "out in semis" score — both semifinal losers play the 3rd-place match and take scores 3 and 4 from the result.
